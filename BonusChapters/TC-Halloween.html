The Carver: Halloween Special

Morgan

I laid out my utensils on the kitchen island, awaiting Cole's return, waiting to start the operation. Taking each scalpel and each dagger, I check whether they have been adequately sharpened, sharp enough to cut through flesh smoothly. Everything was ready, except one thing was missing, the victims.

After about ten minutes of waiting, I heard the key turn in my front door, followed by familiar footsteps; heavy yet calculated. “I brought three, as you asked.” Cole’s voice echoes down my hallway.

“Bring them into the kitchen, I have everything prepared here.” I shout in his direction.

“Won’t you mess up your entire kitchen?” He asks as he enters the kitchen, carrying the three pumpkins.

“I can clean it up,” I say as I watch him plop them onto the cabinet. “I’m experienced.”

He laughs. “Yeah, but usually you burn down whole buildings in order to clean a single room.”

“This room is fully tiled; I should be fine. More importantly, why didn’t you take a bag instead of carrying those massive pumpkins.” I have a whole collection of tote bags that he could’ve utilised instead of carrying those things, it bewildered me .

“Morgan, it was an extra charge.” His response has me almost speechless.

“You’re a fucking billionaire!”

“Yeah, but carrier bags should be free; I’m protesting.”

“Ok, I can get behind that, that said I still have like fifty or more tote bags.” I argue.

“Morgan… you are a serial killer, an assassin and a hacker,” He counts out all my occupations on his fingers. “Yet somehow, all your tote bags are pink and cutesy. Let me have some dignity.”

“I’m just a girl. Besides, you could make pink work.” I nudge him playfully as I arrange each pumpkin into its respective place.

“Just because you can pull off anything doesn’t mean everyone else can too.” He jokes as he walks over to the nearest pumpkin. “Where’s Danielle?”

“She has a job interview,” I explain “So it’s just me and you today.” I start preparing all the necessary tools I’m going to need to create my masterpiece. This included pulling up a photo on my phone for reference.

Cole looks at his pumpkin thoughtfully . “I never celebrated Halloween. I’m thirty-two and have never done anything for Halloween, including carving pumpkins. My dad always put up a sign saying: we don’t celebrate Halloween; whenever some poor kid – who most likely hadn’t learned to read yet – would knock on the door, he’d scream at them.” Cole reminisces.

“Is that why you bought ten different packs of sweets?” I ask, he nods silently in response. Cole is currently learning more about his dad, connecting more dots together and realising how much shit he went through as a child, teenager and young adult.

The sombre look leaves his face and he turns to face me. “Your nickname is The Carver ; I can only assume you’re extremely skilled at carving pumpkins.”

I turn to him with a serious look upon my face. “Cole, people like you, who stereotype, they always put people like me in little boxes. You did pick the right box though; I have won many awards in my life in pumpkin carving competitions,” The seriousness leaves my face. “It got so bad – for them, I mean – that they had to ban me from participating, in an attempt to give the other kids the opportunity to win.” I laugh remembering all the plastic trophies that are displayed in my parent’s house, as well as all the tears the other kids shed whenever I won. Good times.

Cole starts to laugh too. “Was that one of the key moments in your life that prompted your goals and current job?”

“Yes,” I reply calmly, Cole laughs even harder in response. There was a moment I genuinely I believed he was about to piss himself laughing. “Alright, would you like to start first?” He nods and picks up the knife, but instead of slicing into the orange flesh he just stares at the pumpkin.

I pick up my scalpel and start carving out little indents, each one at a different depth to create shading and details. I look over at Cole who still hasn’t started on his pumpkin.

He sighs. “I don’t know where to start.”

“Okay, have you decided on a design?” I ask him gently, he stares blankly at the pumpkin. I took the lack of answer as a no. “Alright, well let’s start there, go on the internet and search for some pumpkin carving inspiration.” Cole pulls out his phone and searches for exactly that. “I’d recommend you go for a simple triangle eyes, square nose, zigzag mouth.” I point to an image on the screen that fitted my description. “I wanted to do something more detailed,” He complains childishly. “Like a portrait of you.” I stifle a laugh. “Cole, sweetheart, it took me a decade to get comfortable enough to even attempt a proper painting-like pumpkin carving. I say it with love when I say: please, stick to the basics.” I chuckle softly and kiss him on the forehead, his suggestion did give me an idea though. “Ok, I’ll do a regular pumpkin face,” He says begrudgingly. “What do I do next?” “I’ll grab a pen for you, you can draw everything out so that you know where to cut.” I open the junk drawer and grab a random pen and test if it works, scribbling several times. “But you’re not using a pen.” I sense him trying to catch a peek at what I’m doing. “Yes, because I’m experienced,” I hand him the pen that had the most ink. “I know how to use these tools to manipulate the pumpkin into looking exactly how I want it to.” I explain simply to him, I pick up my scalpel again and return to carving. The room falls silent as both of us focus on carving our separate pumpkins. I see Cole drawing out all the features. It’s a small thing, but seeing this intimidating-looking man with his tongue out, drawing out eyebrows and what seems to be a moustache on a pumpkin, is a sight I never expected to witness. I go a little too deep with one of my cuts, I try to save it by blending it out into the surroundings. Cole is still deeply invested in his pumpkin but I see it coming along. After an hour of silent carving, Cole finally speaks. “I think I’m done.” I finish the incision and turn to look over at his pumpkin. To my utter shock, he may have been hiding a natural pumpkin-carving talent all this time. It looked incredible. “Wait, I love it.” I get up to grab a candle to light the pumpkin, excited to see the final result . He scoffs. “You can’t talk, Morgan, just look at your masterpiece.” “Yes, but I have also been doing this for twenty-five years,” I bite back as I open the top that Cole had already taken the liberty of cutting off. I light the candle before placing it in the pumpkin. “Congratulations, Mr Mortalis, you have successfully carved your first pumpkin.” I rub his shoulder, quietly proud of him. “Does this mean I also get to call myself The Carver?” He asks with a smirk. “No, there can only be one Carver.” I wink at him, returning to my own pumpkin. After half an hour passes , I hear the door unlock. Cole had blown out the candle since it had started to burn the inside of his pumpkin. I’ll need to drown this pumpkin in resin so that it can become a keepsake, I thought to myself . I hear light and cheery footsteps before Danielle appears in the doorway, a sharp contrast with Cole’s. “And? How did it go?” I ask her, although her demeanour already tell me everything I need to know. “I got the job!” She exclaims, squealing . I jump from my seat and run up to her, giving her the tightest hug, congratulating her. “Well done, Danielle.” Cole responds from the island. “Thank you, guys! I’m so happy.” I pull away from her, she’s so smiley. Danielle observes the mess spread about the kitchen. “I see you’re carving pumpkins, has Morgan showed you the pumpkins she has made throughout the years? Her parents shared a folder with me.” She says as she starts pulling up Google Drive . “No, don’t, my face is in all of those photos.” I visibly wince, clawing for the phone. “Show me immediately.” Cole flies from his seat, sending the barstool flying backwards. Danielle jumped back while Cole ran up to her. I wanted to argue further, but this man’s desperation to see shut me up as it was all just too entertaining. “You had braces?!” Cole was in momentary awe. “Well yeah, obviously, have you not seen how straight my teeth are?” “I never really paid your teeth that much attention ,” His gaze returns to Danielle’s phone. “You really did earn the nickname The Carver, Morgan, these are insane.” “This is last year.” Danielle swipes on the screen, showing him yet another photo. He grabs it off of her and zooms in even closer on the image, like a sixty-year-old who forgot their glasses. “Is that the fucking Michelangelo sculpture with Danielle’s face on it?” “I was very proud of that artwork.” I finalise the smaller touches on my pumpkin. “In fact, I was so proud I turned it into a painting and gifted it to Danielle for her birthday that very year.” “Can you do that with my pumpkin?” He asks with genuine pleading in his eyes. “Please, please, please?” “Sure, it’ll be pretty easy since it’s just a pumpkin.” I accept his request, his toothy grin lights up his face. “Cole, stop smiling like that, you’ll make me fall in love with you.” After another hour of working on my pumpkin, I finally finish. Offputtingly, Cole looks at it funnily. “It looks like a bunch of scratches.” Cole says, trying to figure out what I made. “Move, you uncultured swine.” I brush off the remark and place a candle inside, magically revealing the artwork. “Christopher as a king?” Danielle starts laughing uncontrollably. “It’s our Lord and saviour, His Majesty Christopher.” I announce dramatically. I had carved my Pomeranian into the pumpkin, stylising so it looked like he was wearing a beautiful crown and royal regalia. Cole starts laughing once he realises what is going on, on the pumpkin. We sat around a little longer, talking shit until Cole realised he had to leave since he starts work early tomorrow. “Dani, my bestie, I have a query.” I turn to my best friend. I ask Danielle if I could use her pumpkin for my idea, that we could do our usual pumpkin carving session tomorrow. She was more than happy to indulge me, as that meant more pumpkins for decoration and more time we could spend time together. I spent the whole night in the kitchen, carving away. Cutting, shaving, shading endlessly . I kept a photo open on my phone for reference to make sure I didn’t miss a single detail. I repeatedly shone a flashlight from inside the pumpkin, ensuring everything would light up the way I wanted it to when the candlelight hit it. Around 4am, I was finally done with my magnum opus . I packed it inside a sturdy box, wrote a small note and stuck it onto the box, and drove to Cole's house. I left it outside his door. Justifiably afterwards, I went back home and fell asleep.

Cole

I drag myself to the front door. My Ring camera had recorded movement around 4am and Adam had mentioned there was a package there waiting for me. It was checked over by a few of my colleagues just in case it was something suspicious, clearly it had been given the green light as they had left it there for me to grab. I groan as bring the surprisingly heavy package inside, letting it thud on a coffee table, the cardboard box stared back at me almost insulted by its treatment . I notice little note on the top, so I peel it off and read it. Dear Cole, I appreciated your suggestion last night, so I decided to put together a little present for you to enjoy. From, Your favourite psycho. The cursive handwriting immediately gave away the mysterious sender, making me smile. Without opening the box, I take it to the privacy of my office to reveal its contents alone. Inside, a pumpkin. The design seems to be a bunch of blobs and purposeful scratches at a glance, but I know better than to judge a pumpkin by the carving alone now. I reach into a drawer and pull out a single tealight. I light it up and set it up comfortably inside, revealing the magical transformation . I blink several times to assure myself that my eyes are not lying, but they’re not and this is the partial nude that Morgan sent me months ago. I take in every fantastic detail, the lace on her lingerie is distinguishable from the ribbons and sequins and all the other minute details. I’m trembling, smiling and drooling like an idiot ; she even cut the skin of the pumpkin to illuminate her tattoos. I touch the piece delicately; afraid I may ruin something somehow. The detail is so meticulous I can feel it under my fingers. That I can feel her under my fingers. I place the pumpkin at the front of my desk hoping that Morgan wouldn’t mind, it felt only right to send her a thank you note and a photo of the pumpkin on display in all its glory. She didn’t respond right away, I assumed it had something to do with her being awake until 4am carving her highly memorable curves into orange skin, judging by the Ring camera timestamp. It was around noon that I finally got a response from Her Majesty , detailing that how glad she is that I like the pumpkin, followed by the infamous reference photo. I put my phone down abruptly as Adam walks in. “What the fuck is that?!” He looks at the pumpkin and begins to touch it, I almost smacked his hand away. “T-that’s an actual carving? T-that’s fucking insane, how does someone do that?” Adam struggled for words the same way I struggled to comprehend it too at first. “She is insane.” I emphasise each word, believing each of them too, I gently slap the pumpkin like a faithful dog. Once he had finished admiring Morgan as a pumpkin pin-up , Adam filled me in on any news in the field and what there is to do. Morgan sends me an invitation to her house to lend her a hand giving kids candy; surprisingly something I have never done. My dad refused to acknowledge Halloween so this will be interesting. Apparently, I will need to dress up. I finish work a little early and dress like a stereotypical wealthy vampire ; one of the perks in being a Don is I have a great deal of suits to choose from. Next, I apply some foundation in a shade a little lighter than my natural skin tone. I grab a cheap make-up sponge dipped in black face-paint and dab it around my eyes, following it with a wet paintbrush dipped it in the same face-paint, drawing ghostly veins around my eyes. As a final touch, I grab a small tube of fake blood and let it dribble down from my lower lip down to my chin. While this fake blood won’t fool anyone with half a brain, I’ve seen the real thing and it this really is fake blood. Happy with the end result, I make my way over to Morgan’s place. I knock on Morgan's door, and to my surprise, a glitzy witch opens the door. “Say it.” She rasps her voice comically. “Say what?” I respond a little confused but then it hits me, my face droops. “I’m not saying it.” “Do it.” I sigh, admitting defeat. I don’t argue, I just say it. “Trick or treat.” “Good boy.” She cackles and hands me a neon-coloured lollipop. She really was a witch. A part of me felt a twinge, perhaps even healed. I open up the piece of candy and put it in my mouth. “You did an amazing job, I’m genuinely impressed,” She takes in my guise. I’m an adult man in my mid-thirties, I’m a boss of hundreds of people who are rightfully terrified of me , but this, this is making me blush. “We probably have like two to three hours until the kids start walking around, Dracula.” I narrow my eyes and immediately correct her. “It’s Count for you, young lady.” She raises her eyebrow at me. “As you wish, Count.” She adds in a comical little bow and continues leading me to the kitchen where there are two bowls on the counter, piled sky-high full of candy. What caught my attention the most was the out of place smell. “Who are we giving the liver and rice to?” I ask, noticing the bubbling pot on the stove. “That’s for the kids with shitty costumes.” Danielle responded, seated at the counter scribbling something down on a piece of paper. “Okay, here’s the vegan candy and non-vegan candy, separated.” “Yes, because segregation has never had a negative impact.” I say off-handedly, Morgan chokes on her own shock and laughter. After a couple of minutes, we heard a knock at the door and I ran to open it, eager to try my hand at door duty. On the porch, there were three toddlers: one chubby-cheeked vampire, one Frankenstein with a snotty nose and a corpse bride who was about to burst into tears . They looked at me wide-eyed and quietly chorused, “Trick or treat.” At that moment, I realised I had left the candy in the kitchen. “Morgan!” I call out behind me, worried I was fucking this interaction up already. “I’m right behind you.” I hear her rapid footsteps approach from behind as she fed the two bowls with expert skill from through the loops of my arms. Unfortunately, the children couldn’t see her so they jumped back a little, frightened by the unexpected trick they had unwittingly asked for. “Here you go guys, Happy Halloween!” I took the bowls from her and offered them to the teary-eyed monsters, they muttered a small thank you and toddled away with fists full of candy. “How does it feel?” Morgan asks.

“Like I can kiss you at any moment.”

Safe to say it was the best first Halloween I had ever experienced; even if I didn’t get to kiss the pretty witch.
